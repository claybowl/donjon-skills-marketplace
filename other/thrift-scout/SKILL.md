# Thrift Store Scouting Guide

Maximize thrifting efficiency with intelligence on what to hunt, where to go, and when to shop. Your personal thrifting strategist.

---

Turns random thrifting into strategic hunting:

## Store Intelligence by Chain

### Goodwill

**Best Locations:**
- Affluent suburbs (higher-end donations)
- Near universities (trendy student turnover)
- College towns (vintage, band tees)

**Restock Schedule:**
- **Monday-Wednesday**: New merchandise processed from weekend donations
- **Thursday-Friday**: Peak inventory days
- **Saturday**: Best selection but most crowded
- **Sunday**: Markdown day at many locations

**Pro Tips:**
- Color tag sales rotate weekly (check store calendar)
- Electronics section often overlooked
- Books: Check first editions, vintage cookbooks
- Furniture: Solid wood pieces, mid-century finds

**What to Skip:**
- Fast fashion (H&M, Forever 21) - low resale value
- Synthetic athletic wear unless vintage
- Shoes with heavy wear
- Modern costume jewelry

---

### Salvation Army

**Best Locations:**
- Urban centers with mixed demographics
- Near retirement communities (vintage, quality)
- Warehouse-style stores (largest selection)

**Restock Schedule:**
- Daily restocking, but **Wednesday** typically best
- 50% off Wednesdays at many locations

**Pro Tips:**
- Furniture section often underpriced
- Vintage housewares and kitchen items
- Men's section overlooked (good for workwear)
- Check jewelry case for sterling silver

---

### Local/Independent Thrift Stores

**Best Types:**
- Charity-run shops (hospice, church, animal rescue)
- Estate sale overflow stores
- Vintage-focused boutiques

**Advantages:**
- Curated selection
- Knowledgeable staff
- Better pricing than chains
- Unique local items

**Strategy:**
- Build relationships with staff
- Ask about new arrivals
- Check back frequently (small inventory turnover)

## Regional Intelligence

### High-Yield Areas

**Affluent Suburbs:**
- Designer labels more common
- Higher quality donations
- Less competition from resellers
- Better condition items

**University Towns:**
- Trendy, current styles
- Fast turnover (semester ends)
- Band tees, streetwear
- Tech accessories

**Retirement Communities:**
- True vintage pieces
- Quality construction
- Classic styles
- Lower prices

### Regional Pricing Norms

| Region | Pricing | Best Finds |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Northeast (NYC, Boston) | Higher | Designer, vintage |
| Midwest (Chicago, Detroit) | Moderate | Workwear, vintage |
| South (Atlanta, Dallas) | Lower | Designer accessories |
| West Coast (LA, SF, Seattle) | Higher | Outdoor brands, tech |
| Pacific Northwest | Moderate | Outdoor gear, flannel |

## Day-of-Week Strategy

### Monday
- **Best for**: First pick of weekend donations
- **Skip**: Still processing, incomplete selection
- **Strategy**: Hit stores that process Sunday donations

### Tuesday-Wednesday
- **Best for**: Fresh merchandise, less crowded
- **Target**: Newly processed items
- **Strategy**: Best days for serious hunting

### Thursday-Friday
- **Best for**: Full selection, weekend prep
- **Target**: Items other resellers missed
- **Strategy**: Good for quick scans

### Saturday
- **Best for**: Maximum inventory visible
- **Skip**: Crowded, picked over by afternoon
- **Strategy**: Go early (store opening)

### Sunday
- **Best for**: Markdown items, relaxed browsing
- **Target**: Color tag sales, clearance
- **Strategy**: End-of-week deals

## Scouting Workflow

### Step 1: Pre-Trip Planning

**Check before leaving:**
1. Weekly hit list updates
2. Store sale calendars
3. Weather (affects donations and crowds)
4. Time available (affects number of stores)

**Pack:**
- Phone for research/photos
- Measuring tape
- Cash (some stores cash-only)
- Hand sanitizer
- Reusable bags

### Step 2: Store Arrival Strategy

**Upon entering:**
1. Scan color tag sales (if applicable)
2. Note busy sections to avoid/visit
3. Check new arrivals rack first
4. Prioritize high-value sections:
   - Men's jackets/workwear
   - Dresses (designer potential)
   - Accessories/jewelry case
   - Shoes (if good condition)

### Step 3: Item Evaluation

**For each potential find:**
1. Check brand label
2. Assess condition (stains, pilling, damage)
3. Verify material (natural fibers worth more)
4. Check comps on phone (eBay sold listings)
5. Calculate profit margin
6. Decision: Buy / Research More / Pass

### Step 4: Post-Trip Documentation

**After each trip:**
- Photo inventory of purchases
- Note total spent
- Track mileage (tax deduction)
- Update inventory spreadsheet

## Common Gotchas & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Store picked over | Try different location or day; wealthier areas restocked more |
| Prices too high | Wait for color tag sales; check outlet/bin stores |
| Reseller competition | Go early morning; build staff relationships |
| Inconsistent quality | Stick to high-yield zip codes; estate sales |
| Can't find specific items | Be patient; thrifting is numbers game |
| Overwhelming selection | Focus on 2-3 categories per trip |

## Safety & Boundaries

- **Personal safety**: Shop with buddy when possible, especially bins/warehouses
- **Hygiene**: Wash all purchases before listing
- **Budget discipline**: Set spending limit before entering store
- **Quality standards**: Don't buy damaged items "to fix later"
