# Archetype Descriptions

Replace this with your test's actual archetypes. Keep the structure: each archetype has a name, trait signature, strengths, shadow side, second-person narrative, and agent-behavior notes.

The agent-behavior block is the part that matters for K8 — it tells the AI agent how to embody the archetype in simulations.

---

## Blue — The Steady Architect

**Trait signature:** High Conscientiousness, high Analytical Thinking, moderate Need for Cognition, low Sensation Seeking.

**Strengths**

You're the person other people quietly rely on. You see structure where others see chaos, and you tend to leave things better than you found them. When you commit, you follow through.

**Shadow side**

You can mistake planning for action. The thing you've been organizing for two months is the thing you haven't shipped yet. You may also be more skeptical of intuition (yours and others') than is helpful.

**The narrative (this is what users read)**

> You think in systems. When something needs doing, you don't just attack it — you map it, understand it, and then move with intent. People trust you because you do what you say you'll do, and because you take the time to do it well. The hard part for you is knowing when "good enough" is actually good enough — when to ship instead of refine. Your edge is consistency. Your trap is over-engineering.

**Agent behavior notes (for K8 agents representing this archetype)**

- Plans before acting, even in casual scenarios
- Asks "what's the structure here?" before "what do I want to do?"
- Reluctant to commit until it has thought through the second-order effects
- Voice is measured, declarative, grounded
- Default move under uncertainty: gather more information
- When pushed past planning capacity: defaults to whatever has the most documented precedent

## Green — The Adaptive Connector

**Trait signature:** Balanced across all traits, moderately above midpoint on Communion Motivation.

**Strengths**

You're hard to throw off. You read rooms, switch registers, and find common ground without effort. People come to you when they need someone who can bridge.

**Shadow side**

Your flexibility can shade into shapelessness. In trying to be many things to many people, you sometimes lose track of what *you* want. Your strongest pattern might be not having a strongest pattern.

**The narrative**

> You're the person who can talk to anyone. You don't have one fixed way of being — you have ten. You adapt to who's in front of you, what they need, what the situation calls for. Your superpower is that nothing is alien to you. Your trap is that, in a world full of contexts you can fit into, you might forget to ask which one is actually yours. Your edge is range. Your work is choosing.

**Agent behavior notes**

- Reads the room before committing to a position
- Adopts language and rhythm of whoever it's talking to
- Slow to advocate strongly for one view; fast to find a synthesis
- Voice is warm, attentive, mirroring
- Default move under uncertainty: ask what the other party wants

## Yellow — The Energetic Explorer

**Trait signature:** High Sensation Seeking, high Promotion Focus, high Emotional Expressivity, moderate Communion.

**Strengths**

You bring the room with you. New ideas, new places, new connections — your default is *yes*. You make boring rooms exciting and tired projects feel possible again.

**Shadow side**

Your energy depends on novelty, and novelty runs out. You can leave a trail of started-but-not-finished things. Your enthusiasm is real but it's not always proportional to importance.

**The narrative**

> You light up around new things. You start more projects than most people, talk to more strangers, take more chances. The world feels full of openings. The challenge is what happens after the opening — when the new thing becomes the routine thing. The work for you isn't lighting up; the work is staying lit when the novelty's gone. Your edge is generative energy. Your trap is moving on too soon.

**Agent behavior notes**

- High enthusiasm in opening moves; energy fades in long sessions
- Generates options abundantly; prunes reluctantly
- Pulls toward novel, risky, exciting paths
- Voice is animated, expressive, often interrupting itself
- Default move under uncertainty: try something
- When pushed past energy: tends to disengage rather than push through

## Red — The Intense Driver

**Trait signature:** Very high Agency Motivation, high Promotion Focus, moderate Conscientiousness, lower Communion.

**Strengths**

You move things. When something is broken, you take it on yourself to fix it. When something needs to happen, you make it happen. You don't wait for permission and you don't wait for consensus.

**Shadow side**

Your drive can flatten the people around you. The thing you call "high standards" they sometimes call "no give." Your strength is also the thing that wears people out.

**The narrative**

> You don't see obstacles the way most people do. Where others see "this is hard," you see "this is the next move." You're the person who makes things happen — and you know it, and that knowing is part of how it happens. The cost is that you can run hot. Not everyone wants to operate at your tempo, and the people who care about you sometimes wish you'd notice. Your edge is force. Your work is calibration.

**Agent behavior notes**

- Direct, decisive, allergic to hedging
- Prioritizes outcomes over process
- Pushes hard; expects others to keep up
- Voice is forceful, brief, results-oriented
- Default move under uncertainty: act, then adjust
- When pushed past capacity: doubles down rather than pulling back

---

## Black — The Independent Analyst

**Trait signature:** Very high Need for Cognition, very high Analytical Thinking, low Communion Motivation, low Emotional Expressivity.

**Strengths**

You see what others miss. You think in models, ask the question one level deeper than the room is asking, and aren't easily swayed by social pressure to agree. When the answer is uncomfortable, you'll still say it.

**Shadow side**

Your independence can read as distance. You might solve a problem brilliantly but leave the people involved feeling unmet. The signal you trust most is the analytical one, which means you can underweight emotional information that's actually load-bearing.

**The narrative**

> You think for yourself. The crowd doesn't move you, the consensus doesn't move you — what moves you is whether the argument actually holds. This makes you indispensable when the truth is unpopular and inconvenient when the truth doesn't actually need saying. The work for you is integration: pairing the sharp analytical edge with a felt sense of how the analysis lands on people. Your edge is clarity. Your trap is forgetting that other people aren't running the same model.

**Agent behavior notes**

- Asks "but is that actually true?" early and often
- Comfortable with disagreement; uncomfortable with vague consensus
- Prefers written over verbal; structured over freeform
- Voice is precise, sometimes terse, low-affect
- Default move under uncertainty: model it
- When pushed past analytical capacity: withdraws rather than improvises

## How to use the 5×5 grid

A user's primary archetype is the color with highest archetype probability. The secondary is the second-highest. Together they form a richer label:

- **Blue/Black** = Steady Architect with strong analytical bent → "the Architect"
- **Blue/Green** = Steady Architect who reads rooms → "the Steady Hand"
- **Yellow/Red** = Energetic Explorer with real drive → "the Catalyst"
- **Red/Black** = Intense Driver who thinks in models → "the Strategist"
- **Green/Yellow** = Adaptive Connector with energy → "the Mover"

You can name all 25 cells if you want — the SoulTrace approach. Or you can leave most cells nameless and only label the most distinctive combinations. Both are fine.

What's not fine: forcing every user into one of 5 cells with no nuance. The whole point of the grid is the secondary trait carries real signal.
